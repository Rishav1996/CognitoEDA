The highly statistically significant difference in mean glucose levels between individuals with different outcomes indicates that glucose is a primary and robust predictor of the outcome. BMI as a Contributing Risk Factor

While not as strong as glucose, the statistically significant positive correlation between BMI and the outcome suggests that higher BMI contributes to the likelihood of the outcome.
